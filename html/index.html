HTML: The Basics

HTML: The Basics


<!-- The doctype indicates what DTD to use, 
that is is considered valid HTML -->
<!DOCTYPE html>
<!-- All web pages must have a root HTML node -->
<html lang="en">
  <!-- head tag provides meta information for the document 
  and is not rendered -->
  <head>
    <!-- inside the head tag you can have meta information, 
    such as keywords, description, etc -->
    <meta charset="utf-8">
    <!-- this is the title of the page, very important for seo, 
    appears on the top of the browser -->
    <title>HTML: The Basics</title>
    <!-- people often include scripts and stylesheets in the head -->
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="css/style.css">
    <script src="js/scripts.js"></script>
  </head>
  <!-- all content for the web page goes into the body -->
  <body>
  </body>
</html>

Semantic HTML

What is wrong with this markup?


<body>
  <div>Electronic Arts Posts A Record Quarter</div>
  <div>All thanks to Origin</div>
  <div>With the launch of great programs such as on the house, 
  as well as several blockbusters ...</div>
  <img src="titanfall.jpg" alt="">
  <div>Games Coming Next Fall:</div>
  <div>- Titanfall 2</div>
  <div>- Battlefield x++</div>
  <div>- Fifa date(Y)</div>

A Little Better


<body>
  <h1>Electronic Arts Posts A Record Quarter</h1>

  as well as several blockbusters ...</p>
  <img src="titanfall.jpg" alt="">
  <h2>Games Coming Next Fall:</h2>

Semantic Navs

Almost all navigations are in the following format.


<div> <!-- div just for a wrapper -->
  <ul> <!-- a nav is really a list of links -->
    <li><a href='#'>Link 1</a></li>
    <li><a href='#'>Link 2</a></li>
  </ul>
</div>

Semantic Headers

Be careful of what you decide is an header. Just because something should appear visually big, does not mean it is a heading. You should be able to look at your list of headers and get an overview of the document.


<h1>Electronic Arts Posts a Record Quarter</h1>
<div>All thanks to origin</div>

Schema.org

Schema.org

Schema.org defines a set of entities that you can define in your markup to give search engines more information about the content on your website.

Help Ye Browsers


<div class="product-info" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Product">
  <h1 class="product-heading" itemprop="name">Essential Charcoal Suit</h1>
  <div class="product-price" itemprop="offers" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Offer">
    <span itemprop="price">$449</span>
  </div>
  <link itemprop="image" href="productimage.jpg">
  <link itemprop="url" href="/product/essential-charcoal-suit">
  <p class="product-short-desc" itemprop="description">
    The perfect everyday suit. Timeless, wearable and versatile 
    for any occasion.</p>
</div>
